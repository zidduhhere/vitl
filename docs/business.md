# VitalLink — Business Slide Content

Formatted for direct use in slides: short bullets, not paragraphs. A suggested single "on-screen" business slide is marked **CORE** — everything else is backup detail for speaker notes or Q&A defense if you have more slide budget.

---

## Slide: Market — CORE

**Headline option:** "India already built telemedicine. We built what's missing."

On-screen bullets:

- eSanjeevani: 340M+ consultations, 131,000+ centers, free, government-run
- Built on one assumption: a working internet connection
- Its own documentation states this plainly — internet is the only prerequisite
- The gap: monsoon-disrupted, high-loss, low-bandwidth conditions where that assumption breaks
- VitalLink is the resilience layer for exactly that residual gap

Speaker note (say, don't put on slide): _"We're not replacing India's telemedicine infrastructure — we're the layer that keeps it working exactly where and when it currently fails."_

---

## Slide: Who This Serves (backup, or fold into Impact slide)

- **Field health workers** — carry the client device, capture and send vitals
- **Specialist doctors** — receive live vitals + patient EHR context
- **State Health Departments / NHM** — primary institutional buyer
- **NGOs** — early-adopter deployments in connectivity-fragile districts
- **Private hospital rural outreach programs** — B2B licensing target
- **Disaster response / defense medical corps** — adjacent vertical, same core problem

---

## Slide: Business Model — CORE (if you have a dedicated business slide)

**Headline option:** "Infrastructure, not an app — licensed like one"

On-screen bullets:

- B2G: licensed to state health departments as a resilience add-on, not a platform replacement
- B2B: embeddable SDK for existing telemedicine platforms — "resilient transport as a service"
- NGO pilots: grant-funded early deployments that become case studies for larger contracts
- Hardware + software bundling: pre-loaded low-cost devices for field programs

---

## Slide: Recurring Revenue — CORE (can combine with Business Model slide)

**Headline option:** "Recurring by design"

On-screen bullets:

- Per-device / per-field-worker subscription (SaaS seat licensing)
- Usage-based pricing — per session or per MB reliably transmitted
- Annual Maintenance Contracts (AMC) — standard in Indian govt/enterprise procurement
- Doctor-side dashboard seat licensing, per hub or per specialist

---

## One-liner for the "Ask" or closing slide

_"340 million consultations proved India wants telemedicine. We built the layer that makes it work where the network can't be trusted."_

---

## Slide Count Guidance

If your business section is a **single slide** (typical for a hackathon pitch), use only the Market bullets + Business Model bullets, trimmed to 4–5 total lines. Recurring revenue and stakeholder detail move to speaker notes, ready to state verbally or answer if a judge asks — don't try to fit everything on-screen at once. Dense slides read as unpolished regardless of how good the content is.
